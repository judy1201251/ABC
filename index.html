Ginger Duck

Ginger Duck (薑母鴨) is a traditional Taiwanese dish made primarily with duck meat, ginger, scallions, chili, and spices. The name "Ginger Mother" refers to the large amount of ginger used, giving the broth a spicy yet mild flavor. This dish is known for its rich soup and tender duck meat, and is often enjoyed as a warming, nourishing meal, especially in cold weather. It is commonly paired with sake or rice wine to enhance the flavor, and can be served with side dishes like poached chicken or cold salads.

Tangyuan

Tangyuan is a traditional Chinese sweet food typically enjoyed during the Lantern Festival or Winter Solstice. It is made from glutinous rice flour, shaped into small balls, and filled with sweet fillings like red bean paste, sesame, or peanuts. Tangyuan is cooked in hot water until soft and tender, offering a chewy texture and a sweet taste. The sticky rice dough and sweet filling complement each other perfectly, making it a delicious treat that is often enjoyed with sweet soup or sugar water.
